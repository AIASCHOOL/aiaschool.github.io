Junior College Concurrent Course

AIA Aichi International Academy & ASU Aichi Sangyō University
Simultaneous Enrollment, Dual Registration

Reduce hassle
The enrollment procedures for the junior college are handled by this institute.

  The Aichi International Academy Japanese Language Department Enrollment Course (2 years) and the Aichi Sangyo University Correspondence Education International Communication Department (2 years) allow simultaneous enrollment and graduation. Students can enroll in both schools without an entrance exam, and it is possible to study university courses even without passing the Japanese Language Proficiency Test N2 level. 

  In the two-year Japanese language course at Aichi International Academy, students can acquire 8 credits. Additionally, without attending a university, they can earn 54 credits through reading reports and three subject screenings alone, totaling 60 credits to receive a junior college graduation certificate and degree.

reduce the time.
It is possible to shorten the required study duration of two years.
Typically, it takes 4 years to complete both Japanese language school and junior college graduation. However, in this course, it can be completed in 2 years.
　Upon graduation from this course, students can either be recommended for or pass the entrance exam to transfer to the third year of a Japanese university. Therefore, while it usually takes 6 years to graduate from a Japanese language school and a 4-year university, this course allows students to graduate in 4 years.
Saving on tuition fees
It is possible to save approximately 5 million yen in expenses.
As seen in the comparison of tuition fees, living expenses, and required study periods on the right, the typical learning process takes 6 years from Japanese language school to university graduation, with a total cost of approximately 15 million yen. In contrast, the college dual enrollment course at this school allows completion in 4 years with a total cost of around 10 million yen. In other words, it is possible to save about 2 years of time and effort, as well as approximately 5 million yen in expenses.
Be a professional
Numerous career paths are available

After graduating from Japanese language school
Japanese language proficiency is comparable to university graduates, and qualification for employment in Japanese companies is also possible.
After completing junior college
Able to obtain a junior college degree and specialized knowledge, making it possible to work in Japanese companies.
After graduating from a four-year university
After graduating from a four-year university, there are numerous career options such as pursuing graduate studies, entering the workforce in Japanese companies, or starting one's own business.

Those who are determined and unwavering in their pursuit of knowledge will eventually succeed.

　In 2015, through the introduction of an English teacher, I came to Japan and began studying Japanese at Aichi International Academy. During that time, I learned about the short-term concurrent course and, with the guidance of the school, visited a university before deciding to apply. Initially, I was unsure if I could keep up with the studies, but with the careful support of the school, I was able to obtain two graduation certificates from the college and junior college. Subsequently, I established a company in Nagoya, started a real estate investment business, and fulfilled my life's dreams in a foreign environment.

　My impression upon applying for the short-term concurrent course is, 'Under heaven, all is clear; I fear only the hearts of men.' I was greatly impressed by the responsible handling of the institute and the enthusiastic and meticulous teaching attitude of the university professors.
Earning Credits
Over two years at this institute, I obtained 8 Japanese language credits, 42 credits through reading reports for 21 subjects, and 12 credits for each of the 6 subjects through lectures, totaling 62 credits and earning a junior college degree.
Total of 62 credits earned, obtaining an associate degree from the junior college.
Transfer Achievements
After graduating from junior college, besides transferring to Aichi Sangyo University for a 3-year program, it is also possible to transfer to other universities or to enter employment with Japanese companies.
