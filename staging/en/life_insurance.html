Student Insurance

National Health Insurance / Comprehensive Insurance for Foreigners

To create a secure environment for students to study, all students are required to enroll in both National Health Insurance and comprehensive insurance for foreigners. This coverage aims to alleviate the financial burden associated with high costs resulting from accidents, death, disability, illness, and other unforeseen circumstances, ensuring that students can focus on their studies with peace of mind.
National Health Insurance
Creating a Relieved Study Environment

　National Health Insurance is a universal healthcare system based on the Japanese National Health Insurance Act. It is designed to cover residents of Japan, including both Japanese nationals and foreign residents with permanent residency status.
　As for insurance premium payments, the amount varies depending on income, but in the first year for foreign students, the insurance premium is approximately 2,000 yen per month. Subsequently, it may increase or decrease depending on income.
　Generally, the personal burden for medical expenses is 30%, and individuals with low income facing high medical costs may have the remaining amount covered by the government, with a monthly cap set at 35,400 yen.

Comprehensive Insurance for Foreigners
Relieved Even in the Event of Accidents or Disasters
This insurance service is designed for foreign students, ensuring immediate assistance in case of unforeseen events. All students are required to enroll in this insurance throughout their period of enrollment.
